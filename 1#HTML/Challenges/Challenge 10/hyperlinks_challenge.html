What I have learnt

My HTML experience started off quite straight-fowardly. It started out with me learning about how HTML tags influence how a web browser interprets how your information should be displayed. These tags give the web browser specific instructions as to where it should go and what it actually is. This for me was really enlightening, as it gave me a glimpse as to how computers operate.

Let's talk headings. To specify that an element should be a heading, a simple [h] tag will suffice. Now, the way headings work is -- they run from 1 to 6, with 1 being the most important and biggest and 6 being the least important ans the smallest heading. This concept is really simple and should be understood without much effort.

Then we have paragraphs. They have the same tag function, but unlike headings, they do not require you to specify their importance as paragraphs have the same level of vitality. They are initiated with a simple [p] tag.

Information can be listed. So, when it comes to listing we have the ordered and the unordered list. Both of them start out with the [li] tag. Now, for a unordered list, you further specify a [ul] tag and for a ordered list, you further specify a [ol] tag.

Time for images. When comes to importing images onto a webpage, you can either use a url link or a directory from your personal server. It's much safer to link images from your personal server as the site that you're linking from might break and therefore stop your image from being displayed.

Do you need your data tabulated? If so, HTML has a table function that is started with a [table] tag. Furthermore, your table is divided into columns and rows -- so you'll a few more tags. Those would be the [tr], [th] and [td] tags. This would be a little too much to explain in one go, so contact me for further clarification.

Also, there will be forms to be made -- especially if your an entrepreneur looking to gather data about what your customers' preferances are and what improvements you as a business owner can make.



HTML intiles many things -- many more things that I will be learning further into the coming future. As for now, there a few other things I know.
